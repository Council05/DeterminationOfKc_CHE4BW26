## How It Works
By flooding a known solution of KSCN with Fe<sup>3+</sup> ions, it can be assumed that all of the ions form the desired FeSCN<sup>2+</sup>. The relationship between FeSCN<sup>2+</sup> and absorbance of light (at 447nm) can be found. When a mixture of known concentrations of KSCN and Fe<sup>3+</sup> ions is brought together, the concentration can be measured. Since all of the KSCN and Fe<sup>3+</sup> ions do not form FeSCN<sup>2+</sup>, the equilibrium constant of the complex ion formation can be determined.

## Results
The accuracy of this procedure is mixed. There is no clear equilibrium constant determined for this salt under these conditions in the literature, and the precision of this procedure is rather poor due to the fact that we are recording absorbance near the edge of what the machine is capable of measuring.

## What I Learned
- Flooding a solution with one reactant can mean you assume that the other reactant is fully consumed
- Absorbance is used as a proxy for concentration

## Future Improvements
Better calibrated lab equipment could be used, or choose concentrations of reactants that would form sufficient FeSCN<sup>2+</sup> such that absorbance readings are in the confident measuring capabilities of the current equipment.
